Hope It Floats Rules - 2016
  • Competitions
    1. There will 4 Pre-Competitions and a Draft of Materials. See Section 5.
    2. There will one 4 person+ boat competition with multiple heats. The winners of each heat advance to the finals. See section 3.
      1. HIF reserves the right to advance more than one boat per heat based on the number of participants. See rule 3.d.
    3. A committee representative will be present to handle any questions regarding rules and materials on the day of the big race. Any questions prior can be directed to Jenny Feldman.
    4. The Rules Czar, responsible for any rules decisions, will be announced the morning of the event. The Czar will not be a member of any team.
  • Teams
    1. Teams will have a minimum of 4 persons to assist with planning, building and racing. The team may choose the 4 or more people that will race the boat.
  • The Race
    1. A mininimum of four members of the team must travel the length of the pond and back.
      1. Each boat must carry a minimum of 4 participants down and back across the bond.
      2. The combined weight of the 4 participants must be no less than ~600lbs.
      3. Each boat must carry a minimum of 4 participants down and back across the pond.
      4. If your 4 racers do not equal the minimum weight then you will be allowed to add additional racers (as many as you would like), or additional weight such as sandbags of cinder blocks to cover the limit.
    2. Team members may not use hands or feet for propulsion.
    3. While all members of the team must travel the length of the pond and back, they do not have to do so on the same trip.
    4. Depending on total number of teams registered for each competition, teams may be divided into heats within their competition, which will be assigned through a random process. The top finishers from each heat will advance to the championship round, which will take place at the conclusion of the heats.
      1. Number to advance will be determined on the day of the contest based on participation.
      2. The HIF Planning Committee reserves the right to adjust participants in any heat based on participants that are not able to start the race.
  • Boat Materials
    1. Teams may use any item in the construction of a boat that is not in the exceptions list described in section 4.b.
    2. MATERIAL EXCEPTIONS:
      1. Any item that was designed to be used as part of a boat.
      2. Any item that was designed for the purpose of floatation.
      3. Any item that is or contains styrofoam.
      4. Any item that is inflated.
      5. Any item that contains toxins or other materials that are harmful to lakes.
        • The HIF Planning Committee reserves the right to disqualify any boat that uses any harmful or illegal materials at any point in the context.
        • For clarification or questions about a material contact Jenny Feldman.
    3. Any item awarded to a team by the Planning Committee is not subject to the requirements of Rule 4.a or 4.b.
    4. Items may be acquired at any time before and during the competition until the conclusion of the construction period.
  • Draft of Materials and Pre-Competitions
    1. There will be 4 Pre-Competitions, the first beginning at 9am.
    2. The Pre-Competitions for HIF 2016 will be revealed and explained the morning of the race.
    3. No preparation or materials are needed for the Pre-Competitions.
    4. The results of the Pre-Competitions will determine the draft order for the Draft of Materials.
    5. The Draft of Materials will have multiple rounds, each in the same order, until all the materials have been assigned or chosen.
    6. Some of the materials may be used to hinder another team, these will be identified to you by the HIF Planning Committee during the draft.
  • Construction
    1. Construction of boats will begin immediately following the Draft of Materials.
    2. Construction time will be limited to THREE hours!
      1. In the event of the reuse of materials from previous competitions, all previous work must be deconstructed prior to the construction period, unless acquired pre-assembled through Draft of Materials.
    3. All construction must take place on the premises of "The Cove," the site of the competition.
    4. There will be a short period where repairs are allowed between the heats and the championship round.
    5. During the construction period, no boats are allowed into the water for testing or any other purpose. With 30 minutes to go in the construction period, teams will be allowed to put boats in the water for testing purposes and any repairs/adjustment may be made during that time.
    6. Official Construction Start, Test, and Stop times will be announced on day of race and any changes to schedule will be announced to each team. Subject to change based on inclement weather.
  • Clean Up
    1. All teams are responsible for disposing of building materials by Sunday of the Hope It Floats weekend, or before they leave the premises, whichever may come first. Nothing is to be left at The Cove.
